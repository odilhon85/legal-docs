1. Information We Collect

We categorize the information associated with the App into the following types:

1.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Google Account Information Optional — If you choose to sign in with Google to use cloud backup features, we receive your Google account email address through the Google Sign-In API.
  • Scanned Documents — Images, PDFs, and text extracted from documents you scan using the App.
  • User-Created Content — QR codes, barcodes, document annotations, signatures, and exported files you create within the App.
  • Feedback Emails — If you contact us via email, we receive your email address and the content of your message.

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Analytics Data — We use Firebase Analytics to collect usage data such as feature usage events (e.g., scans completed, exports performed, filters applied), screen views, and app interactions. This data is collected in an aggregated, anonymized form.
  • Crash Reports — We use Firebase Crashlytics to collect crash logs, stack traces, and device state information when the App encounters an error. Crashlytics is disabled in debug builds and only active in production releases.
  • Advertising Data — Google AdMob may collect device identifiers, IP address, and interaction data to serve and measure advertisements. See Section 4: Advertising for details.

1.3 Information We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect your name, phone number, or physical address.
  • We do not collect precise or coarse location data.
  • We do not access or read your contacts.
  • We do not record audio or access your microphone.
  • We do not track you across other apps or websites.
  • We do not sell your personal data to any third party.

4. Advertising

The App displays advertisements provided by Google AdMob to support free access to app features. The following ad formats are used:

  • Banner Ads — Displayed at the bottom of certain screens.
  • Interstitial Ads — Full-screen ads shown periodically between actions (with a minimum 60-second cooldown).
  • Native Ads — Ads integrated into document lists, clearly labeled as "Ad."
  • App Open Ads — Displayed when the App returns to the foreground (with a 4-hour cooldown).

4.1 Data Collected by AdMob

Google AdMob may collect and process the following data to serve personalized or non-personalized ads:

  • Device identifiers (e.g., Android Advertising ID)
  • IP address
  • Device information (model, OS version, screen size)
  • Ad interaction data (impressions, clicks)
  • App usage data

For more information on how Google uses data from apps that use its services, visit: How Google uses information from sites or apps that use our services.

4.2 Ad-Free Experience

You can remove all advertisements by purchasing the Pro upgrade (one-time in-app purchase). Once the Pro upgrade is purchased, no ad-related data will be collected by AdMob.

4.3 Ad Personalization

You can opt out of personalized advertising by adjusting your device settings:

  • Go to Settings > Google > Ads on your Android device.
  • Enable "Opt out of Ads Personalization" (or "Delete advertising ID" on Android 12+).

5. Google Sign-In & Google Drive

5.1 Google Sign-In

The App offers optional Google Sign-In to enable cloud backup features. When you sign in, we request access to the following:

  • Email address — Used solely to identify your account within the App and display it in settings.
  • Google Drive file scope (drive.file) — A restricted scope that limits our access to only files created by the App. We cannot access any other files in your Google Drive.
Important: We use the most restrictive Google Drive scope available (drive.file). The App can only read, write, and manage files that it has created in a dedicated "MoreThanScan" folder within your Google Drive. We have no access to your other Drive files, folders, or shared drives.

5.2 Google Drive Usage

If you enable cloud backup, the App will:

  • Create a dedicated folder named "MoreThanScan" in your Google Drive.
  • Upload scanned PDF documents to this folder (manually or via automatic backup).
  • Download your backed-up documents for restoration.
  • Optionally compress documents before upload.
  • Optionally restrict uploads to Wi-Fi connections only.

Automatic backup, if enabled, runs approximately every 6 hours using Android WorkManager and operates in the background with a persistent notification.

5.3 Revoking Access

You can disconnect your Google account and revoke Drive access at any time from within the App's settings. You may also revoke access directly at Google Account Permissions.

6. Camera & Image Data

The App requires camera access to perform its core function of document scanning.

6.1 How Camera Data Is Used

  • Live Preview — Camera frames are processed in real-time for document edge detection and auto-capture. This processing is performed entirely on-device using OpenCV.
  • Image Capture — When you capture a document, the image is saved locally in JPEG format in the App's private internal storage.
  • Image Processing — All image operations (perspective correction, cropping, filtering, enhancement) are performed locally on your device. No images are sent to any server unless you explicitly choose to upload them to Google Drive.

6.2 Photo Library Access

The App may request access to your device's photo library to allow importing existing images for scanning. This access is only used when you explicitly choose to import an image. On Android 13 and above, the App uses the READ_MEDIA_IMAGES permission; on older versions, READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE is used.

7. OCR & Text Recognition

All text recognition is performed entirely on your device. No document text, images, or OCR results are sent to any external server for processing.

The App uses Google ML Kit for on-device text recognition, supporting the following scripts:

  • Latin (English and other Latin-script languages)
  • Chinese
  • Japanese
  • Korean
  • Devanagari (Hindi, Sanskrit, etc.)

ML Kit models are downloaded to your device on first use. Once downloaded, text recognition works fully offline without any network connection.

Similarly, barcode and QR code scanning using ML Kit is performed entirely on-device.

8. In-App Purchases

The App offers a one-time Pro upgrade purchase through Google Play Billing. When you make a purchase:

  • The transaction is processed entirely by Google Play. We do not collect or store your payment information (credit card numbers, billing address, etc.).
  • We receive a purchase token from Google Play to verify your Pro status.
  • Your Pro status is stored locally on your device and can be restored via the "Restore Purchases" feature.

9. Data Storage & Security

9.1 Local Storage

Your scanned documents, images, OCR text, and app settings are stored locally on your device in the App's private internal storage directory. This data is not accessible to other apps.

The App uses:

  • Encrypted Database — Document metadata is stored in an encrypted Room database using Android Security Crypto library (androidx.security:security-crypto).
  • Private File Storage — Scanned images and exported files are stored in the App's internal file directory (filesDir), which is sandboxed by the Android operating system.
  • Encrypted Preferences — App settings are stored using Android DataStore with encryption support.

9.2 Document Password Protection

The App allows you to set a password on individual documents. Passwords are securely hashed before storage — the original password text is never stored.

9.3 Cloud Storage

If you choose to use Google Drive backup, your documents are stored in your own Google Drive account under a dedicated "MoreThanScan" folder. The security of this data is governed by Google's Privacy Policy and your Google account security settings.

9.4 Security Measures

  • All network communications use HTTPS/TLS encryption.
  • Google Sign-In uses industry-standard OAuth 2.0 protocol.
  • Local database is encrypted using Android Keystore-backed encryption keys.
  • The App supports biometric authentication for additional protection.

11. Data Retention

  • Local Data — Your scanned documents and app data remain on your device until you delete them within the App or uninstall the App. Uninstalling the App removes all locally stored data.
  • Google Drive Data — Documents backed up to Google Drive persist in your Drive account even after uninstalling the App. You can manage or delete these files directly from Google Drive.
  • Firebase Analytics — Analytics data is retained according to Firebase's data retention policies (default: 14 months for event data, 60 days for user-level data).
  • Firebase Crashlytics — Crash data is retained for 90 days.
  • AdMob Data — Advertising data is retained according to Google's Privacy Policy.
